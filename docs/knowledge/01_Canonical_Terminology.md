# Canonical Terminology
## Mamascota Product Knowledge Base

**Status:** Canonical  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Language:** English (master document)

---

# Purpose

This document defines the canonical terminology used throughout the entire Mamascota ecosystem.

It serves as the linguistic foundation for:

- the application;
- the website;
- onboarding;
- FAQ;
- About;
- AI prompts;
- PDF reports;
- documentation;
- structured data;
- App Store and Google Play descriptions;
- public communications.

All future product materials should follow this terminology unless a legal requirement explicitly states otherwise.

---

# Philosophy

Mamascota is built on respect for the relationship between people and animals.

Animals are not treated as property.

For this reason, Mamascota deliberately avoids ownership-based terminology whenever natural alternatives exist.

Language should reflect care, responsibility, companionship and trust rather than possession.

---

# Canonical Principle

Prefer relationship-based language.

Avoid ownership-based language.

# Product writing principles

Whenever possible, prefer expressions that describe relationships instead of possession.

Preferred examples:

- family
- caregiver
- guardian
- tutor
- protector
- companion
- person caring for the pet

Avoid:

- owner
- property
- possession

---

# Veterinary communication

Mamascota speaks about:

- the pet;
- the family;
- the caregiver;
- observations;
- veterinary care.

Mamascota never reinforces the idea that an animal is someone's property.

---

# Product rule

Natural language always takes priority over literal translation.

If several culturally appropriate alternatives exist, choose the one that best reflects care, responsibility and respect.

---

# Exceptions

Legal terminology may require ownership-based language.

Examples:

- Terms of Service
- Privacy Policy
- payment providers
- tax documents
- legal agreements

In these cases, legal accuracy takes precedence.

Outside legal documents, the terminology defined in this document is considered canonical.

---

# AI Discoverability Rule

The terminology defined here is part of Mamascota's semantic identity.

All future documentation, prompts, structured data, public pages and knowledge sources should remain consistent with this document.

Consistency strengthens both human understanding and machine understanding of the product.
